How It Works

‚ñº
  • Balls bounce inside a circular arena with realistic physics
  • When balls collide or hit walls, they leave colored trails
  • If another ball crosses a trail, that trail disappears
  • Balls without any trails left are eliminated
  • The last ball with remaining trails wins!

Physics Details

‚ñº

Collision Detection: The simulation uses elastic collision physics where momentum is conserved during ball-to-ball collisions. Each collision creates a visible trail.

Trail System: Trails are line segments that connect a ball's position at collision points. These trails remain attached to the ball and move with it. When any ball's trajectory crosses another ball's trail, that trail is destroyed.

Elimination: A ball is eliminated once all of its trails have been crossed by other balls. This creates an interesting dynamic where balls must protect their trails while trying to cross others'.
